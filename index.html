About me

I'm Sakib and I'm a

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Saskatchewan. I completed my M.Sc. in Biomedical Engineering from the same university. I developed ways to detect machine learning models to predict Autism patients from the healthy ones using brain MRI images during my M.Sc. studies. However, at the time of the development, I realized that deep learning models are considered black boxes, and there is very little understanding of how it makes a decision. To find these answers, I am working with Explainable AI in my Ph.D. I am developing ways to describe the decisions of deep learning models and make them more accessible and trustworthy for people without the proper domain knowledge. I am leveraging my experience in computer vision and graph theory to open the black box.
